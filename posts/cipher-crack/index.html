Cipher Crack

2020-04-02

I started this project while working on the Southampton university cipher challenge as I wanted an easy way of testing words my team and I thought were likely to be the passphrases, also I wanted the ability to leave this running over long periods of time in an attempt to brute force it by using a dictionary attack.

The initial cipher text is decoded with a passphrase, either from a file of words (such as a dictionary) or from a predefined function, such as trying all possible words of length 6. The output from this is then rated on the amount of times a list of words come up and if desired how closely the text resembles English (would be easy to change for other languages using the latin alphabet) using the index of coincidence. A combination of these two factors is then used to come up with a rating, then once the list of strings is completed or all possibilites for the function are exhausted all outputs with their respective keys are outputted in order of which is most likely to be the plaintext.

Currently only three ciphers are implemented: Hill cipher, Transposition and Vigenere, however it would be easy to add your own cipher.
